Key findings

01 You're paying security-tier rates for basic support

Twelve months of tickets are mostly password resets, printer issues, and new-employee setup — routine Tier 1 work — yet the contract is priced and marketed as "24/7 monitoring and security."

02 The security you're paying for isn't fully there

On Business Standard, the environment lacks Intune, Conditional Access, and Defender for Business. There's no true EDR, and MFA isn't enforced on all accounts. Marketed protection and deployed protection don't match.

03 Your "backup" is not a backup

The MSP bills $600/month for "backup and DR," but it's default OneDrive sync — no point-in-time recovery, no protection from mass deletion. We asked the provider to demonstrate a restore; they could not produce evidence any restore test had ever been performed.

04 Offboarding gaps create real exposure

No documented offboarding process. A terminated employee's account was found still active three weeks after their last day — a live security and licensing exposure.

05 You don't fully own your own environment

Documentation and admin credentials live with the MSP, not with you. If the relationship changed tomorrow, transition would be difficult and dependent on the outgoing provider's cooperation.
